How It Works
SECTION TWO
Hydraulic Vortex Regulation

The swirl
does the work.

Vortex flow control uses the natural hydraulic properties of swirling water to regulate flow rates without the need for moving parts. The system generates rotational energy inside the chamber, creating a controlled vortex. As stormwater enters, centrifugal forces restrict the water flow, and the vortex strength increases in response to higher inflow rates.

The result is a consistent, pre-set maximum discharge rate, even under varying storm conditions. This mechanism is crucial in flood prevention and helps manage the capacity of downstream infrastructure by reducing peak flow during heavy rainfall events.
